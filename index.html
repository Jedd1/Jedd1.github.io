Why I like the McLaren P1

I love the McLaren P1 because of what a beast of a car it is. Sleek, fast and a looker too. It really has it all that a car needs. It is just a beautiful car.

About the Mclaren P1

The P1 features a 3.8 litre twin-turbo V8 engine. These boost the petrol engine at 1.4 to deliver 727 bhp.

The productions version of the McLaren P1 was inveiled at the 2013 Geneva Motor Show. Production to 375 units which according to McLaren, is to maintain exclusivity. McLaren announced a sub seven minute lap of the Nurburgring Nordschleife which equates to an average speed in excess of 111mph.

The Bugatti. What a car.

Speed,Style... there is an unlimited amount of words I can use to describe the fastest roadworthy car on the planet. The Bugatti really does have it all.

Facts and Specs

The Bugatti Veyron's top speed is 268mph, the fastest for a roadworthy car. Top Gear named the Bugatti Veyron as the best car of the decade in 2009. Unfortunately Bugatti Veyron manufacturing was discontinued in 2014. The Bugatti company have since released the Bugatti Chiron.

The Aston Martin DB5

Being a Bond Fan I generally love all things to do with it and naturally I will love the DB5. It has achieved iconic status and is the most well known car in the whole of the Bond film series, as well as being one of the most well known cars of all time.

Some specs and facts for you

The engine is a 3,995cc inline-6, it's top speed is 143mph and it does 0-60 in 8s. Only 1059 Aston Martin DB5's were produced meaning that they are extremely valuable and difficult to get.

Jaguar E-Type

One of the most memorable cars of the sixties and seventies, the Jaguar E-Type. ts combination of beauty, high performance and competitive pricing established the model as an icon of the motoring world. Upon its release in 1961 Enzo Ferarri described the E-Type as the most beautiful car ever made.

About the E-Type

In 2008 the Jaguar E-Type ranked first in the Telegraph's 100 most beautiful cars of all time. Land Rover Defender, why I love it

One of the Land Rover's flagship cars the Land Rover defender was a favourite for all Land Rover fans. Produced since 1983 it was a sad day when it ceased being produced on the 29th of January 2016. It even had a Top Gear episode dedicated to it with Richard Hammond driving it up a dam.
